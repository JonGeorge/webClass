How to Encrypt your FileSystem with FIPS Enabled
------------------------------------------------
Problem:  The encryption option (during install) encrypts with FIPS disabled
          If you enable FIPS, then you cannot boot your CentOS installation

Solution:
 1) Enable FIPS
 2) Encrypt Volumnes

References
----------
https://unix.stackexchange.com/questions/226661/enabling-fips-on-a-luks-encrypted-centos-system-breaks-boot-password/226822#226822
http://geekswing.com/geek/how-to-encrypt-a-filesystem-on-redhat-6-4centos-6-4-linux-fips-or-no-fips/


Assumptions:
 A) You have not encrypted your volumes yet
 B) You do not have FIPS enabled


Part 1:  Enable FIPS
---------------------
 1. Check if FIPS is enabled
    unix> cat /proc/sys/crypto/fips_enabled
    0 = not enabled
    1 = enabled

 2. Turn off prelinking
    unix> vi /etc/sysconfig/prelink

          Change
          PRELINKING=yes
          to
          PRELINKING=no
